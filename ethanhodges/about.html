When I was a little kid I had an intense love of reading. My parents tried really hard to hide all of the flashlights in the house. I ended up needing to use toys with dim blinking lights. I remember using a Bionicles sword to read, I'm suprised that I don't need glasses now. When I was in second grade, my teacher gave up on me. I was super ADHD, the only thing that would shut me up was reading books. She would give me a huge pile every day and let me read in the corner. I just took a bunch of those reading comprehension tests every day. Now, my reading retention is through the roof but I can't do math to save my life. Since third grade I loved to create worlds and stories, but these never had any structure. In fifth grade, I wrote a tiny short story, my teacher loved it. I remember that it was about a ghost that kidnapped a kid, fun stuff. I copied the style of the Series of Unfortunate Events books. I read so many books, but slowed down after discovering video games. Once I got back from my mission I started to read again. Last year, I kept track, I finished over 50 books! I'm practicing writing every day now, it's hard, but rewarding.

It would be so cool, but I probably need to practice more to win.
